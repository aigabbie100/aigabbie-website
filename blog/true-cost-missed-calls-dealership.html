The True Cost of Missed Calls at Your Dealership

Every unanswered ring is money walking out your door. But most dealership owners dramatically underestimate how much.

The Invisible Revenue Leak

You can see when a customer walks out of your showroom. You can track when someone abandons their online shopping cart. But the phone calls that never connect? Those are invisible, and that invisibility makes them dangerous.

Industry research shows the average car dealership receives 300-500 phone calls per week. During peak hours, typically 9-11 AM and 2-4 PM, call volume spikes while your team is busiest with in-person customers. The result: 30-40% of calls go unanswered or abandoned after excessive hold times.

Let's do the math on what this actually costs.

Breaking Down the Numbers

Service Department Calls

Roughly 60% of dealership calls are service-related. These are customers ready to spend money. They just need an appointment.

Average service ticket: $350-$500
Missed calls per week (conservative): 40-60
Service calls in that mix: 24-36

If even half of those missed service calls would have booked an appointment, you're looking at 12-18 lost service tickets per week. At $400 average, that's $4,800-$7,200 in weekly revenue walking out the door.

12 missed service appointments x $400 average ticket = $4,800/week

$4,800 x 52 weeks = $249,600 per year in lost service revenue

Sales Inquiries

The remaining 40% of calls include sales inquiries, and these carry even higher stakes. A single missed sales call could represent a $3,000-$5,000 gross profit on a vehicle sale.

Even if only 2-3 genuine buyers call and can't get through each week, the annual impact is staggering:

2 missed buyers per week x $4,000 average gross = $8,000/week

$8,000 x 52 weeks = $416,000 per year in lost vehicle sales

The Hidden Multiplier: Lifetime Value

These calculations only count the immediate transaction. They don't account for the customer who would have returned for service every 6 months. The referrals they would have sent. The next vehicle they would have purchased from you.

A single lost customer doesn't just cost you one sale. It costs you years of relationship revenue.

When Are You Missing Calls?

The answer might surprise you. While lunch hours and late afternoons see high abandonment rates, the biggest gaps often occur:

After hours: 30% of consumers prefer to call businesses in the evening. Your doors are closed.

Weekends: Saturday and Sunday callers are often ready-to-buy customers with time to shop.
